🎤 Pitch / Prosodic Identity (Glottal Source Characteristics)
----------------------------------------------------------------
f0_mean → Captures the singer’s habitual pitch range (natural vocal register signature).
f0_std → Measures expressive pitch variation (degree of gamaka/vibrato usage).
f0_min → Indicates lowest phonation limit, reflecting vocal weight and tessitura.
f0_max → Indicates upper singing capability and stylistic pitch excursions.

🎼 MFCCs — Vocal Timbre Envelope (Primary Singer Identity Marker)
----------------------------------------------------------------
MFCCs approximate the spectral envelope shaped by the vocal tract, which defines timbre.

For each coefficient:

mfcc1_mean → Overall spectral slope (brightness vs warmth of voice).
mfcc1_std → Stability of vocal tone over time.
mfcc2_mean → Formant structure linked to vowel coloration unique to singer.
mfcc2_std → Variability in articulation style.
mfcc3_mean → Captures nasality vs openness in resonance.
mfcc3_std → Reflects stylistic modulation of resonance.
mfcc4_mean → Encodes singer-specific vocal tract shaping.
mfcc4_std → Measures consistency of that shaping.
mfcc5_mean → Represents mid-frequency coloration (playback tone color).
mfcc5_std → Captures expressive tonal shifts.
mfcc6_mean → Sensitive to throat configuration and support.
mfcc6_std → Variation in projection dynamics.
mfcc7_mean → Encodes fine timbral individuality.
mfcc7_std → Reflects stylistic articulation changes.
mfcc8_mean → Tracks subtle harmonic shaping unique to singer.
mfcc8_std → Measures control over tonal texture.
mfcc9_mean → Captures micro-spectral personality cues.
mfcc9_std → Expressive variation in those cues.
mfcc10_mean → Higher-order timbral fingerprint component.
mfcc10_std → Stability of that fingerprint.
mfcc11_mean → Encodes nuanced vocal color differences.
mfcc11_std → Reflects modulation richness.
mfcc12_mean → Captures high-frequency resonance behavior.
mfcc12_std → Measures brilliance variation.
mfcc13_mean → Represents fine harmonic detail tied to identity.
mfcc13_std → Stability of these micro-characteristics.

🔊 Spectral Shape Features (Energy Distribution Signature)
----------------------------------------------------------------
centroid_mean → Perceived “brightness” of voice (dark Ilayaraja vs brighter SPB).
bandwidth_mean → Spread of harmonic energy, linked to vocal fullness vs sharpness.
rolloff_mean → Frequency below which most energy lies (defines tonal weight).
flatness_mean → Measures harmonic richness vs breathiness/noisiness.

🔉 Loudness / Phonation Control Features
----------------------------------------------------------------
rms_mean → Average vocal power reflecting projection style.
rms_std → Dynamic control and expressive loudness variation.

⚡ Temporal Excitation Feature
----------------------------------------------------------------
zcr_mean → Measures excitation sharpness, distinguishing smooth vs edgy phonation.

🎶 Harmonic Quality Feature
----------------------------------------------------------------
hnr (Harmonic-to-Noise Ratio) → Quantifies clarity of phonation vs breathiness, a strong cue of singer tone quality.

NOTES:
---------
“Singer identity is governed by both the excitation source (pitch behavior) and the vocal tract filter (spectral envelope).
The selected features jointly model pitch dynamics, resonance structure, spectral energy distribution, and harmonic quality — which are the same dimensions used in speaker recognition and music information retrieval to characterize voice individuality.”

Because identity is not a single frame — it is a distributional behavior over time, so we compare statistical signatures of voices rather than instantaneous values.
